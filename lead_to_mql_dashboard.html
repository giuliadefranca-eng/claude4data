The FY27 Q1 "CVR Collapse" Is Mostly a Composition Story

Blended lead-to-MQL CVR dropped from ~12% to 2.8% in Feb 2026 — alarming at first glance. But after excluding outbound Sales leads (0.2% CVR), event invite-only leads (0% CVR), and customer leads, marketing-sourced CVR dropped from ~18% to ~9% — a real decline, but not catastrophic. 76K non-marketing leads flooded the denominator in Q1 alone. Paid, Direct, and Organic channels remain stable at 11–16% CVR. The remaining "Other" channel is the gap.

4. System Auto-Close — $7.2M Lost Pipeline

Demo & Trial MQLs Are Dying in Queue

3,003 Demo MQLs and 1,689 Trial MQLs were auto-closed in the last 15 months. Of those, 1,499 Demo and 1,147 Trial MQLs never even reached SAL — never touched by a rep. Meanwhile, Livechat (0.4% auto-close) and Contact Sales (2.1%) have near-zero auto-close because they use real-time routing. The fix: replicate Livechat's instant-assignment model for Demo & Trial form submissions.

Issue 1: 231 "Regular Booth" Leads Are Dead on Arrival

231 attendees with "Attended Booth" (non-High Intent) have a 0.4% MQL rate. Among them: Bitdefender (6 leads), Customer.io (some), Chime, AppsFlyer, GitHub, NYSE, PagerDuty — all ICP-matched accounts. Without a post-event nurture to create a second touchpoint, these leads are permanently parked. Recommendation: Auto-enroll all booth attendees into a 90-day post-event nurture sequence targeting a demo/trial CTA.

Issue 2: 103 "High Intent" Attendees Still Didn't MQL

103 "High Intent" attendees failed to MQL despite strong engagement. Includes Five9 and Forethought (High Intent), plus Customer.io (5 leads). Possible causes: existing customer filtering, active opp exclusion, or MQL-Event scoring requiring additional criteria beyond "High Intent" status. Recommendation: Audit the 103 to determine if they're blocked by a scoring rule vs a data issue. Even converting 10% = 10 incremental MQLs from qualified accounts.

Issue 3: 125 Leads Have No Account = Invisible

125 badge scans (26% of members) have a Lead ID but zero Account ID — never matched by LeanData. No account = no ICP, no segment, no industry, no scoring. These are small/unknown companies from the tradeshow floor. Recommendation: Run Clay enrichment on the 125 to create/match accounts. Makes them scorable and routes them into ICP-based nurture paths.

9. Priority Actions

1. Fix the Metric: Exclude Non-Marketing Leads

Add filters to the lead-to-MQL metric: exclude channel = 'Unattributed' AND first_person_source = 'Sales', first_person_source ILIKE 'Event invited%', and first_person_source = 'Customer'. This removes ~76K leads/quarter that were never in the marketing funnel. Effort: low (filter change). Impact: accurate signal.

2. Fix Demo/Trial Auto-Close Routing

2,646 high-intent MQLs (Demo + Trial) were never touched by a rep and auto-closed. Estimated $7.2M lost S2 pipeline, ~$1.8–2.2M lost bookings/yr. Replicate the Livechat/Contact Sales real-time routing model for form-based MQLs. LATAM needs priority capacity review (19–20% auto-close).

3. Review Content Syndication Vendors

Infuse and TechAdvice campaigns produce 58–73% DQ rates. These leads pass MQL scoring but fail sales acceptance — wasting SDR review time. Options: raise scoring thresholds for content synd sources, add ICP pre-filters to vendor contracts, or reallocate spend to higher-quality channels.

4. Investigate Marketing CVR Decline

Even after filtering, marketing-sourced CVR dropped from ~18% to ~9% in FY27 Q1. Paid/Direct/Organic channels are stable at 11–16%. The gap is in "Other" (events, content, referrals) — needs upstream trace by program.
